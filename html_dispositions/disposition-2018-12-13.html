  Motions

Council Motions

# Movers Subject Disposition
1 Sharma, Bowman That Recommendation 1. of Item No. 4 of the Report of the Executive Policy Committee dated December 5, 2018 be amended to read as follows: “That the attached terms of reference for the Human Rights Committee of Council be approved, with the following amendments: Section 5.1 - delete “appointed by the Mayor” and replace it with “appointed by Council, and advertised in accordance with the Council Policy on Citizen Appointments to Boards and Commissions.” Section 10.1 - add the words “for submission to Council for approval” at the end of the bullet.” CARRIED
2 Allard, Santos That a By-law to authorize the proposed local improvement outlined in Recommendation 1 of this report, be enacted in accordance with the attached draft By-law (Schedule "A"), amended as follows: Delete the following: "(a) Asphaltic Concrete Lane Pavement - approximately 16 feet wide (5 inches thick) on the first lane east, between of Ferndale Avenue and Claremont Avenue, from Highfield Street to Coniston Street." and replace with the following: "(b) Asphaltic Concrete Lane Pavement - approximately 16 feet wide (5 inches thick) on the lane in the block bounded by Highfield Street, Coniston Street, Claremont Avenue and Ferndale Avenue." CARRIED
3 Mayes, Lukes That Recommendation 2.A.iii of Item No. 4 of the Report of the Standing Policy Committee on Property and Development, Heritage and Downtown Development dated November 19, 2018 be deleted and replaced with the following: “2.A.iii.That the maximum height of any building shall not exceed 45 feet east of the northeasterly limit of 81 Mardena Crescent (Lot 15, Block 2, Plan 46437).” CARRIED
4 Mayes, Lukes That the 2019 Schedule of Meetings be amended by adding the following meeting dates for the Standing Policy Committee on Property and Development, Heritage and Downtown Development, to consider appeals under the Standing Committee jurisdiction: March 18 May 13 July 15 September 23 November 25 Meetings are to begin at 9:30 a.m. in the Council Building. AUTOMATIC REFERRAL TO THE STANDING POLICY COMMITTEE ON PROPERTY AND DEVELOPMENT, HERITAGE AND DOWNTOWN DEVELOPMENT
5 Eadie, Rollins That the Winnipeg Public Service undertake a consultation with retail outlets, specialty shops and the company that maintains the online bike registry for the purpose of identifying how bicycles can be registered at time of purchase in Winnipeg; That the Winnipeg Public Service report back to City Council with the required amendments to the Doing Business In Winnipeg By-law and plan to implement bicycle registration at time of purchase. AUTOMATIC REFERRAL TO THE STANDING POLICY COMMITTEE ON PROTECTION, COMMUNITY SERVICES AND PARKS
6 Klein, Gillingham That the City Solicitor be directed to prepare a by-law and forward same to City Council to amend the name of the electoral ward of “Charleswood – Tuxedo” to “Charleswood – Tuxedo – Westwood”. That the Proper Officers of the City be authorized to do all things necessary to implement the intent of the foregoing. CARRIED
